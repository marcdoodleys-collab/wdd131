Site Name & Branding

Visit Haiti

The name "Visit Haiti" is simple, direct, and immediately communicates the purpose of the website. It positions Haiti as a tourism destination rather than accepting negative stereotypes that often dominate international media coverage.

Proposed Domain: visithaitiguide.com or discoverhaitours.ht (using the .ht country domain)

Logo: A stylized Haitian flag combined with tropical elements (🇭🇹)

Why this name? This name reflects our mission to showcase Haiti's extraordinary natural beauty and historical significance while challenging misconceptions. It's memorable, SEO-friendly, and appeals to international travelers interested in authentic Caribbean experiences.

Site Purpose

Visit Haiti is a tourism and cultural promotion website for a fictional tour company dedicated to showcasing the most beautiful natural landscapes and historically significant sites in Haiti. The website serves multiple purposes:

  • Educational: Provide information about Haiti's natural wonders (Bassin-Bleu, île à Vache, La Visite National Park, etc.)
  • Historical: Highlight important historical sites such as the Citadelle Laferrière, Fort Liberté, and sites related to the Haitian Revolution
  • Commercial: Showcase tour packages, pricing, booking information, and customer testimonials
  • Cultural Promotion: Counter negative narratives by promoting Haiti's rich heritage and tourism potential
  • Contact & Engagement: Provide ways for potential tourists to contact the company, request custom tours, and sign up for newsletters

Visitor Scenarios

These scenarios represent our target audience and the questions they will have:

Scenario 1: International Adventure Seeker

"I'm looking for an off-the-beaten-path Caribbean destination. What unique natural attractions does Haiti have? What tours are available and how much do they cost? Is it safe for tourists?"

Content needed: Tour packages, pricing, safety information, gallery of natural sites, customer reviews, booking system

Scenario 2: History Enthusiast

"I'm interested in learning about Haiti's role in the Haitian Revolution and African diaspora. What historical monuments can I visit? Are there guided tours focused on Haitian history?"

Content needed: Historical monument descriptions, guided tour options, educational resources, photos of historical sites

Scenario 3: Cultural Tourist

"I want to experience authentic Haitian culture, local cuisine, and meet local communities. What cultural immersion opportunities are available? Can I interact with local artisans?"

Content needed: Cultural experience packages, local cuisine information, artisan collaborations, community-focused tours

Scenario 4: Corporate Group Organizer

"We're planning a company retreat and want something unique in the Caribbean. Can you accommodate a large group? What are the group rates and what activities can we do together?"

Content needed: Group tour packages, custom tour planning, contact form for inquiries, group rates, team-building activities

Color Schema

The color palette is inspired by the Haitian flag and natural landscape, creating a cohesive visual identity that celebrates Haiti's national pride while connecting to its natural beauty.

Deep Ocean Blue (#003d7a)

Usage: Primary color for headers, navigation, section titles, buttons, and footer

Why: Represents Haiti's Caribbean Sea and conveys trust and stability

National Red (#c70000)

Usage: Accent color for call-to-action buttons, important highlights, and decorative elements

Why: Direct reference to the Haitian flag, symbolizes passion and independence

Lush Green (#00a550)

Usage: Secondary accent for hover states, borders, and nature-related content sections

Why: Represents Haiti's tropical vegetation and natural beauty

Sandy Beach (#f4e4c1)

Usage: Background accents and light text backgrounds

Why: Evokes the beautiful Haitian beaches (Bassin-Bleu, île à Vache)

Dark Charcoal (#333333)

Usage: Body text and main content areas for readability

Why: Ensures excellent contrast and accessibility

Typography

A carefully selected combination of serif and sans-serif fonts creates visual hierarchy and enhances readability.

Font 1: Lora (Serif)

Citadelle Laferrière

Usage: Main headings (h1, h2, h3), site title, section headers

Why: Elegant and sophisticated, conveys historical significance and cultural importance

Font 2: Poppins (Sans-Serif)

Body text, navigation, buttons, and call-to-action elements

Usage: Body paragraphs, navigation menus, buttons, forms, secondary headings (h3, h4)

Why: Modern, clean, and highly readable on both desktop and mobile devices. Excellent for web content and accessibility

Font 3: System Fonts (Fallback)

Usage: Quick-loading fallback fonts if custom fonts fail to load

Stack: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif

Website Pages Overview

The Visit Haiti website consists of 5 main pages, each with specific purposes:

1. Home Page (index.html)

The landing page that introduces visitors to Visit Haiti and its offerings.

  • Hero section with compelling headline and call-to-action
  • Featured destinations preview
  • Why Choose Us section
  • Customer testimonials
  • Newsletter signup
  • Links to other pages

2. Destinations Page (destinations.html)

Detailed showcase of tourist sites and attractions across Haiti.

  • Natural attractions (Bassin-Bleu, île à Vache, La Visite National Park)
  • Historical monuments (Citadelle Laferrière, Fort Liberté, Sans-Souci Palace)
  • Cultural sites and experiences
  • Beautiful imagery for each destination
  • Brief description of each site
  • Links to pricing and booking

3. Pricing Page (pricing.html)

Clear pricing information for various tour packages.

  • Tiered pricing packages (Budget, Standard, Premium, VIP)
  • What's included in each package
  • Group rates and discounts
  • Custom tour pricing
  • Booking button for each package
  • FAQ about pricing and payments

4. Contact Page (contact.html)

Contact form and information for customer inquiries.

  • Contact form (name, email, subject, message)
  • Phone number and email address
  • Physical address in Haiti
  • Office hours
  • Social media links
  • Map or location information

This documentation page explaining the website planning and structure.
